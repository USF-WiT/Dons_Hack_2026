About

Get ready for the annual DonsHack, hosted by Women in Tech and the Association for Computing Machinery! This year, it's taking place over the weekend of March 6th-8th, 2026.

We'll be awarding teams with prizes worth over $2000 and we're providing free food and beverages to keep you fueled throughout the weekend. This is your chance to collaborate with your peers, network with alumni, and get personalized guidance from professors in exclusive 1:1 team meetings. We're featuring panelists and judges from environmental tech, AI and more!

We're tackling the theme of Environmental Tech where you'll have the opportunity to build projects that address sustainablility and environmental challenges right here on campus. Create technology for any environmental challenges you see around you! Whether it’s reducing energy consumption, rethinking waste and recycling systems, protecting local biodiversity, or tackling the impacts of climate change, your ideas can help make our campus smarter, greener, and more resilient. Think creatively, act locally, and build solutions that matter.

We're open to all students, regardless of grade, major, or gender, plus it's completely free to participate! This is a fantastic opportunity to add to your resume, and a chance to participate in the largest hackathon offered on campus.

If you have any questions, please contact donshackusfca@gmail.com!

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

Put simply, a hackathon is a chance to build something innovative within a specific theme - for The Dons Hack, that's Environmental Tech! Have an idea that could improve our environment? This is your chance to make it a reality. A hackathon gives you the opportunity to build your portfolio, develop skills, and learn how to work in a team while creating solutions that benefit our USF community.

What is Environmental Tech?

Environmental Tech is our hackathon theme refers to technologies designed to reduce environmental harm and use natural resources more sustainably. It includes innovations like renewable energy, pollution control, waste management, and tools that help monitor and protect ecosystems.This could include tools for improving student learning, campus navigation, resource management, or any other enviornmental solution that benefits the USF community.

Who can participate?

All USF students can participate regardless of major or gender. No prior coding or technical experience is necessary!

Do I have to be there the whole weekend?

While we encourage full participation to get the most out of the experience, you're not required to be present for every moment. However, make sure you're available for key events like the opening ceremony, team formations, final presentations, and closing ceremony.

Do I need to have a team to apply? Is there a limit to the size of each team?

It is not necessary to have a team at the time of registration. We will help you form a team on the first day of the hackathon. If you choose to build a team, there can be a maximum of 4 people. Please make sure each member of your team fills out the registration form!

Do I have to use the prompts you provide?

No! We'll provide prompts that address specific needs from USF departments and campus organizations to help you get started, but you're welcome to come up with your own project idea as long as it aligns with our Environmental Tech theme.

Can I use AI tools in my project?

If you use AI tools (like ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, etc.) in your project, please document which tools you used and how you used them in your project submission. Your project should still demonstrate your own innovative thinking and problem-solving skills.

How many 1:1 sessions can I sign up for? CHANGE THIS

You can sign up for a maximum of two 1:1 sessions with Professor Peterson, Professor Urgo, or alumni Colm Lang, subject to availability. These sessions are a great opportunity to get personalized guidance for your project!

11: 00 AM-1:00 PM: MAGICS with Professor Brizan in KA 309

12:00 PM- 2:00 PM : Machine Learning with Professor Urgo on Zoom

- MUST SIGN UP FOR APPOINTMENT- sign up links can be found in Quick Links

How much does it cost?

The Dons Hack is free for all students! Food and light refreshments will be provided.

What should I bring?

Bring yourself and your laptop!

What are the prizes?

We're offering over $2000 in prizes across these categories: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, Most Potential for Impact, Most Innovative, and People's Choice! The specific prize amounts will be announced during the opening ceremony.
